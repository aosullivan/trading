Trend Ribbon

Trend Ribbon

Adaptive Trend Overlay

Trend Ribbon plots a smoothed center line with an adaptive band that widens as trend conviction strengthens and narrows before potential reversals. Green shading marks bullish structure; red shading marks bearish structure. The strategy is fully in when daily and weekly ribbons confirm bull regime, fully out once daily flips bear while weekly is no longer bullish, and then stays locked out for a cooldown sized to the prior bull cycle.

How To Read It
  • Price holding above a green ribbon suggests bull-regime continuation
  • Compression toward the center line suggests momentum is fading
  • A daily color flip matters most when the weekly ribbon confirms the same regime
Example
BTC's ribbon stays wide and green through a bull leg, then compresses and flips as momentum fades. After a confirmed bear exit, the model waits through a proportional cooldown before accepting the next bull flip, which helps avoid mid-bear fake rallies.
CB50

Channel Breakout 50

Close-Based Breakout (Trend Following Ch.45)

Goes long when today's close equals the highest close over the last 50 days, and exits when it equals the lowest close. An always-in reversal system from Michael Covel's Trend Following.

Calculation
  • HC50 = highest close in last 50 days
  • LC50 = lowest close in last 50 days
  • Buy when Close = HC50
  • Sell when Close = LC50
Example
BTC breaks to a new 50-day closing high at $68k → CB50 goes long. It stays long until price makes a new 50-day closing low, capturing the full trend move.
CB150

Channel Breakout 150

Close-Based Breakout (Trend Following Ch.45)

Same logic as CB50 but with a 150-day lookback. Longer-term variant that historically showed higher returns and Sharpe ratio (0.89 vs 0.56) but less positive skew.

Calculation
  • HC150 = highest close in last 150 days
  • LC150 = lowest close in last 150 days
  • Buy when Close = HC150
  • Sell when Close = LC150
Example
TSLA makes a new 150-day closing high → CB150 enters. This longer lookback filters out more noise than CB50, producing fewer trades with larger average winners.
SMA 10/100

SMA 10/100 Cross

Moving Average Crossover (Trend Following Ch.45)

Long when the 10-day SMA crosses above the 100-day SMA. One of two crossover systems from Covel's backtested trend following framework.

Calculation
  • SMA(10) = average of last 10 closes
  • SMA(100) = average of last 100 closes
  • Buy when SMA(10) > SMA(100)
  • Sell when SMA(10) < SMA(100)
Example
AAPL's 10-day SMA crosses above its 100-day SMA after a pullback → bullish crossover. The fast average accelerating above the slow one signals trend resumption.
SMA 10/200

SMA 10/200 Cross

Moving Average Crossover (Trend Following Ch.45)

Same as SMA 10/100 but with a 200-day slow average. Even slower to turn, filtering more noise at the cost of later entries and exits.

Calculation
  • SMA(10) = average of last 10 closes
  • SMA(200) = average of last 200 closes
  • Buy when SMA(10) > SMA(200)
  • Sell when SMA(10) < SMA(200)
Example
NVDA's 10-day SMA crosses above the 200-day SMA → strong trend confirmation. This is a more conservative filter than the 10/100, staying out of choppier intermediate swings.
EMA Trend

EMA Trend Signal (5-month)

Academic Momentum (Lemperiere et al., Ch.42)

Measures price deviation from its EMA, normalized by volatility. Positive signal = bullish, negative = bearish. Uses a ~105 trading day (5-month) decay rate.

Calculation
  • EMA ref = exponential moving average, span = 105 days
  • Vol = EMA of absolute daily price changes, span = 105 days
  • Signal = (Price − EMA ref) / Vol
  • Buy when Signal > 0; Sell when Signal < 0
Example
ETH trades well above its 5-month EMA with rising signal strength → confirmed uptrend. When price dips below the EMA and signal turns negative, the model exits.
1Y MA

1-Year MA Trend

Trend Filter (Koijen et al., Ch.49)

The trend component of the carry + trend combination strategy. Long when price is above its 252-day (1-year) simple moving average, flat when below.

Calculation
  • SMA(252) = average of last 252 closes (~1 trading year)
  • Buy when Close > SMA(252)
  • Sell when Close < SMA(252)
Example
SPY holds above its 1-year MA through a bull market → stays long. When a correction pushes price below the 252-day average, the model steps aside to avoid drawdowns.
Supertrend

Supertrend (10/2.5)

Trend-Following Overlay

Supertrend is a trend-following indicator plotted on the price chart. It uses ATR (Average True Range) to set dynamic stop levels above or below price, flipping direction when price crosses the band. The tuned default uses 10 periods and a 2.5x ATR multiplier.

Calculation
  • Compute ATR over n periods (default 10)
  • Upper Band = (High + Low) / 2 + Multiplier × ATR
  • Lower Band = (High + Low) / 2 − Multiplier × ATR
  • If close > previous upper band → trend = UP (lower band shown)
  • If close < previous lower band → trend = DOWN (upper band shown)
Example
TSLA closes above the upper band at $245 → Supertrend flips bullish, green line appears below price as a trailing stop. As long as price stays above it, the uptrend is intact.

EMA Cross

EMA Crossover (5/20)

Trend / Momentum

Uses two Exponential Moving Averages — a fast EMA(5) and a slow EMA(20). An EMA gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive than a simple moving average.

Calculation
  • EMA = Price × k + EMA_prev × (1 − k)
  • Where k = 2 / (period + 1)
  • Buy when EMA(5) crosses above EMA(20)
  • Sell when EMA(5) crosses below EMA(20)
Example
AAPL's EMA(5) crosses above EMA(20) at $190 → bullish signal. The faster average is accelerating above the slower one, suggesting upward momentum.

MACD

MACD — Moving Average Convergence Divergence (16/32/9)

Momentum Oscillator

MACD measures the relationship between two EMAs to identify momentum changes and potential trend reversals. It oscillates around zero with no upper/lower bound.

Calculation
  • MACD Line = EMA(16) − EMA(32)
  • Signal Line = EMA(9) of MACD Line
  • Histogram = MACD Line − Signal Line
  • Buy when MACD crosses above Signal Line
  • Sell when MACD crosses below Signal Line
Example
NVDA's MACD line crosses above the signal line while both are below zero → bullish crossover. The histogram flips positive, confirming momentum is shifting upward even though price hasn't broken out yet.

Donchian

Donchian Channel Breakout (10)

Breakout / Volatility

Plots the highest high and lowest low over a lookback period as a channel. Price breaking above the upper band signals bullish breakout; below the lower band signals bearish.

Calculation
  • Upper Band = Highest High over n periods
  • Lower Band = Lowest Low over n periods
  • Buy when close breaks above upper band
  • Sell when close breaks below lower band
Example
BTC consolidates for 10 days between $60k-$64k. Price breaks above $64k (the Donchian upper band) → breakout buy signal. This is the basis of the famous "Turtle Trading" strategy.

BB Breakout

Bollinger Bands Breakout (30/1.5)

Volatility / Mean Reversion

Bollinger Bands plot a middle SMA with upper/lower bands at ±1.5 standard deviations. A close outside signals a volatility breakout, while a break back below the middle band exits the long bias.

Calculation
  • Middle Band = SMA(30)
  • Upper Band = SMA(30) + 1.5 × StdDev(30)
  • Lower Band = SMA(30) − 1.5 × StdDev(30)
  • Buy when close breaks above the upper band
  • Sell when close breaks below the lower band
Example
AMZN squeezes inside narrowing Bollinger Bands (low volatility). Price suddenly closes above the upper band at $185 → breakout signal. The "squeeze" preceding it often leads to explosive moves.

Keltner

Keltner Channel (30/10/1.5)

Volatility / Trend

Similar to Bollinger Bands but uses ATR instead of standard deviation for the band width. This tuned setup uses a slower EMA(30) center and 1.5 × ATR(10) envelopes to smooth noise while preserving breakout responsiveness.

Calculation
  • Middle = EMA(30)
  • Upper = EMA(30) + 1.5 × ATR(10)
  • Lower = EMA(30) − 1.5 × ATR(10)
  • Buy when close breaks above the upper channel
  • Sell when close breaks below the lower channel
Example
META trades within Keltner Channels for weeks. A strong earnings gap pushes price above the upper channel → breakout confirmed. Keltner + Bollinger Band squeeze (BB inside Keltner) is a popular combo for finding explosive setups.

SAR

Parabolic SAR (0.01/0.01/0.1)

Trend / Trailing Stop

Places dots above or below price to indicate trend direction. The dots accelerate toward price as the trend matures, eventually triggering a reversal when price touches a dot.

Calculation
  • SAR_next = SAR + AF × (EP − SAR)
  • AF (Acceleration Factor) starts at 0.01, increments by 0.01 each new extreme, max 0.10
  • EP (Extreme Point) = highest high in uptrend / lowest low in downtrend
  • Dots below price = bullish; dots above = bearish
Example
GOOGL is in an uptrend with SAR dots below price. As the trend extends, dots accelerate closer to price. When price finally dips below a dot → SAR flips bearish, dots appear above. This acts as both a signal and trailing stop-loss level.

CCI

CCI — Commodity Channel Index (30/80)

Momentum Oscillator

Measures how far price deviates from its statistical mean. Despite its name, it works on any asset. Readings above +80 suggest overbought/strong uptrend; below -80 suggest oversold/strong downtrend.

Calculation
  • Typical Price = (High + Low + Close) / 3
  • CCI = (TP − SMA(TP, 30)) / (0.015 × Mean Deviation)
  • The 0.015 constant scales ~75% of values between −100 and +100
  • Buy when CCI crosses above +80 (momentum breakout)
  • Sell when CCI crosses below −80
Example
AMD's CCI surges from -40 to +110 → strong bullish momentum. The reading above +80 confirms price is trending significantly above its 30-period mean, suggesting institutional buying pressure.
